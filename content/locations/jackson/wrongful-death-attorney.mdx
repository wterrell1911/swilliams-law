## Understanding Mississippi's Wrongful Death Statute

Mississippi's wrongful death law provides families with the legal right to seek compensation when a person's death is caused by the wrongful act, neglect, or default of another. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Miss. Code Ann. § 11-7-13 — verify current statutory language, recent amendments, and interaction with survival action under § 11-7-15 */} This statute allows the personal representative of the deceased person's estate to file a lawsuit on behalf of the surviving beneficiaries.

**Who can file:** Under Mississippi law, a wrongful death claim is brought by the personal representative (executor or administrator) of the deceased person's estate. The recovery benefits the statutory beneficiaries, which typically include the surviving spouse and children. If there is no surviving spouse or children, the claim may benefit other heirs such as parents or siblings. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm beneficiary hierarchy under current Mississippi wrongful death statute and relevant case law */}

**Statute of limitations:** You generally have three years from the date of death to file a wrongful death lawsuit in Mississippi. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Miss. Code Ann. § 15-1-49 as applied to wrongful death — confirm no special limitations periods apply */} While this timeline may seem distant in the immediate aftermath of a loss, the reality is that critical evidence begins to deteriorate from the moment an accident occurs. Surveillance footage is overwritten, witnesses relocate, physical evidence is repaired or discarded. Contacting an attorney as early as possible gives us the best chance to build the strongest case for your family.

**Damages available:** Mississippi wrongful death cases can recover several categories of damages:

- **Economic damages** — including the deceased person's lost wages and future earning capacity, medical bills incurred before death, and funeral and burial expenses
- **Non-economic damages** — including the loss of love, companionship, comfort, guidance, and society of the deceased, as well as the pain and suffering the deceased endured before death
- **Punitive damages** — in cases involving particularly reckless, willful, or egregious conduct, Mississippi courts may award punitive damages designed to punish the wrongdoer and deter similar behavior in the future

## Types of Wrongful Death Cases in the Jackson Area

Wrongful death can result from many different types of incidents. In Jackson and the surrounding Hinds County area, Attorney Williams has experience with wrongful death claims arising from:

**Motor vehicle accidents:** The convergence of I-55, I-20, and US-49 in the Jackson metro area creates some of the most dangerous driving conditions in Mississippi. Fatal car, truck, and motorcycle accidents occur on these highways and on local roads throughout Hinds, Madison, and Rankin Counties. When a driver's negligence, intoxication, or distraction causes a fatal collision, the surviving family members have the right to pursue a wrongful death claim.

**Trucking and commercial vehicle accidents:** Mississippi's highways carry heavy commercial traffic, and collisions involving 18-wheelers and other large commercial vehicles are disproportionately likely to result in fatalities. These cases often involve complex questions of federal regulation, carrier liability, and multiple insurance policies. Attorney Williams has the experience to investigate these cases thoroughly and hold all responsible parties accountable.

**Workplace fatalities:** Construction site accidents, industrial equipment failures, and other workplace incidents claim lives in Jackson and across Mississippi every year. While workers' compensation benefits may be available, a separate wrongful death lawsuit may also be appropriate when a third party's negligence contributed to the fatal injury.

**Medical malpractice:** Surgical errors, misdiagnosis, medication mistakes, and other forms of medical negligence at Jackson-area hospitals and clinics can have fatal consequences. These cases require detailed medical expert analysis and a thorough understanding of Mississippi medical malpractice law. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm interaction between wrongful death statute and Mississippi Tort Claims Act for medical malpractice cases, and note any certificate of expert consultation requirements */}

**Premises liability:** Property owners in Jackson have a legal duty to maintain reasonably safe conditions. When a failure to address known hazards leads to a fatal slip and fall, a structural collapse, a drowning, or another preventable death, the property owner may be held liable.

**Defective products:** Manufacturers, distributors, and retailers can be held responsible when a defective product causes a fatal injury. Whether it involves a defective vehicle component, a dangerous consumer product, or a malfunctioning piece of industrial equipment, product liability laws provide an avenue for justice.
