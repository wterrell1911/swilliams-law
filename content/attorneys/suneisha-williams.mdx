## A Personal Mission to Fight for Injury Victims

Attorney Suneisha L. Williams did not choose personal injury law — it chose her.

At 19 years old, while attending the University of Mississippi as an undergraduate, Suneisha was involved in a serious car accident. The experience was life-altering in more ways than one. Beyond the physical injuries and the long road to recovery, she found herself thrust into a legal system she knew nothing about. Insurance adjusters called with settlement offers that seemed designed to confuse. Medical bills piled up. Nobody explained her rights or what she was entitled to.

That experience planted a seed. Suneisha realized that there were countless people — students, working families, individuals without legal connections — who faced the same overwhelming process after an injury. They needed someone in their corner. Someone who understood not just the law, but what it felt like to be the person sitting across the desk, scared and uncertain.

She decided to become that person.

## Education and Early Career

Suneisha completed her undergraduate degree at the University of Mississippi, graduating magna cum laude. She then enrolled at the University of Mississippi School of Law, where she graduated cum laude with her Juris Doctor. During law school, she developed a particular focus on tort law and civil litigation, building the foundation for what would become her life's work.

Born in Chicago and raised in Mississippi, Suneisha's roots span two states — a background that would later prove invaluable when she established a multi-state practice. After passing both the Georgia and Mississippi bar examinations, she relocated to Atlanta in 2013 to open S. Williams Law Firm, LLC.

## Founding S. Williams Law Firm

S. Williams Law Firm was founded on a simple principle: every client deserves to be treated like family. Attorney Williams built the firm from the ground up with the specific mission of representing personal injury and workers' compensation victims across Georgia and Mississippi.

Unlike firms that spread their attention across dozens of practice areas, S. Williams Law Firm focuses exclusively on personal injury and workers' compensation claims. This focused approach means that Attorney Williams and her team handle car accidents, truck accidents, workplace injuries, slip and fall cases, and wrongful death claims day in and day out. This specialization translates into deep expertise, established relationships with medical experts, and a thorough understanding of the tactics insurance companies use to minimize payouts.

## A Courtroom Presence Like No Other

Attorney Williams's courtroom style has been described as "aggressive yet charming and relatable" — a rare combination that serves her clients exceptionally well. She brings fierce advocacy to every negotiation and courtroom appearance, fighting tenaciously for maximum compensation. At the same time, her genuine warmth and relatability connect with juries, judges, and opposing counsel in ways that produce results.

This dual nature is not a strategy — it is who she is. The same attorney who will aggressively cross-examine a defense expert will also answer a client's phone call at 7 PM to ease their concerns about an upcoming deposition. For Attorney Williams, the law is personal because every case represents a real person whose life has been disrupted by someone else's negligence.

## Licensed in Two States

Attorney Williams is licensed to practice in both Georgia and Mississippi, including state and federal courts in both states. This dual licensure allows her to represent clients who are injured in either state, whether the accident occurred on I-285 in Atlanta, on a construction site in Jackson, or on I-20 between the two cities.

Having offices in both Atlanta, Georgia, and Jackson, Mississippi, means that clients across both states have access to experienced legal representation without the inconvenience of traveling to a distant attorney. The firm serves clients throughout the metro Atlanta area — including Fulton County, DeKalb County, Cobb County, and Gwinnett County — as well as throughout central and north Mississippi, including Hinds County, Madison County, Rankin County, and communities such as Tupelo, Oxford, and Batesville.
