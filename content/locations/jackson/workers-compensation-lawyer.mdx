## Understanding the Mississippi Workers' Compensation System

Mississippi's workers' compensation system is governed by the Mississippi Workers' Compensation Law {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Miss. Code Ann. § 71-3-1 et seq. — confirm current statutory provisions referenced throughout this section */} and administered by the Mississippi Workers' Compensation Commission, which is headquartered right here in Jackson. The system is designed to provide benefits to employees who are injured in the course and scope of their employment, regardless of who was at fault for the accident.

**Employer obligations:** Mississippi law requires most employers with five or more employees to carry workers' compensation insurance. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm threshold of five employees under Miss. Code Ann. § 71-3-5 and any recent amendments */} This insurance covers the cost of medical treatment and a portion of lost wages for employees who are injured on the job.

**Benefits available:** If you qualify for workers' compensation in Mississippi, you may be entitled to:

- **Medical benefits:** Coverage for all reasonable and necessary medical treatment related to your workplace injury, including surgeries, prescriptions, physical therapy, and rehabilitation
- **Temporary total disability (TTD):** If your injury prevents you from working, you may receive two-thirds of your average weekly wage, subject to a statutory maximum {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm current TTD maximum weekly rate and calculation method */}
- **Temporary partial disability:** If you can return to work in a limited capacity, you may receive benefits to make up the difference in your earnings
- **Permanent partial disability:** Compensation for lasting impairments that affect your ability to earn a living
- **Permanent total disability:** Benefits for workers who are permanently unable to return to any form of gainful employment
- **Vocational rehabilitation:** Assistance with retraining or job placement if you cannot return to your previous type of work
- **Death benefits:** Compensation for surviving dependents when a workplace injury results in death

**The claims process:** After you are injured at work, you must notify your employer within 30 days. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Miss. Code Ann. § 71-3-35 — confirm 30-day notice requirement */} Your employer is then responsible for reporting the injury to their insurance carrier and to the Mississippi Workers' Compensation Commission. If your claim is accepted, benefits should begin promptly. If your claim is denied or disputed, you have the right to file a petition to controvert with the Commission, and that is where having an experienced attorney becomes critical.
