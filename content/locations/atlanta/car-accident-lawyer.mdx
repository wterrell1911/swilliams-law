## The Scale of Atlanta's Car Accident Problem

Atlanta consistently ranks among the most dangerous cities in the United States for traffic accidents. The Georgia Department of Transportation and the Governor's Office of Highway Safety report thousands of injury-causing crashes in Fulton County alone each year. Several factors contribute to Atlanta's high accident rate:

**Extreme Congestion.** Atlanta's population growth has far outpaced its road infrastructure. The morning and evening rush hours on I-285, I-85, and I-75 create bumper-to-bumper conditions that breed rear-end collisions, sideswipe accidents, and multi-vehicle pileups. What should be a twenty-minute commute from Sandy Springs to Downtown can stretch to well over an hour, and the frustration and impatience this creates leads to aggressive driving behaviors.

**Distracted Driving.** Despite Georgia's Hands-Free Georgia Act, which prohibits drivers from holding or supporting a phone with any part of their body while operating a vehicle, {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: O.C.G.A. § 40-6-241.2 — Verify current provisions of Hands-Free Georgia Act */} distracted driving remains one of the leading causes of accidents on Atlanta's roads. Texting, scrolling social media, using GPS applications, and other phone-related distractions cause drivers to take their eyes off the road at the worst possible moments.

**Speeding.** High-speed corridors like GA-400, I-75 south of the city toward Clayton County, and I-20 through East Atlanta see a disproportionate number of speed-related crashes. When drivers are traveling at excessive speeds, they have less time to react to changing conditions and the force of impact in any collision is dramatically increased.

**Impaired Driving.** Atlanta's vibrant nightlife scene in areas like Buckhead, Midtown, East Atlanta Village, and Edgewood means that impaired driving is an ongoing concern, particularly on weekend nights. DUI-related crashes cause some of the most catastrophic injuries and fatalities on Atlanta's roads.

**Weather Conditions.** Atlanta's frequent summer thunderstorms and occasional winter ice events create hazardous road surfaces that contribute to a spike in accidents during bad weather. Drivers who fail to adjust their speed and following distance for wet or icy conditions are putting everyone on the road at risk.

## What to Do After a Car Accident in Atlanta

The steps you take immediately after a car accident can significantly affect the strength of your legal claim. Here is what S. Williams Law Firm recommends:

1. **Call 911.** Report the accident and request medical assistance if anyone is injured. A police report creates an official record of the crash that will be important evidence in your case.

2. **Seek Medical Attention.** Even if you feel fine at the scene, see a doctor as soon as possible. Many serious injuries, including traumatic brain injuries, soft tissue damage, and internal bleeding, do not produce immediate symptoms. A medical evaluation creates a documented link between the accident and your injuries.

3. **Document Everything.** If you are physically able, take photographs of the accident scene, all vehicles involved, skid marks, traffic signals, road conditions, and your visible injuries. Get the names and contact information of any witnesses.

4. **Exchange Information.** Obtain the other driver's name, phone number, insurance company, policy number, driver's license number, and license plate number.

5. **Do Not Admit Fault.** Be careful about what you say at the scene. Even a simple "I'm sorry" can be used against you later. Stick to the facts when speaking with police.

6. **Do Not Give a Recorded Statement to the Other Driver's Insurance Company.** Insurance adjusters are trained to get you to say things that can be used to deny or reduce your claim. Before speaking with any insurance company, call S. Williams Law Firm at **(404) 504-7027** for guidance.

## Georgia's At-Fault Insurance System

Georgia is an at-fault state for car accidents, which means the driver who caused the accident is financially responsible for the injuries and damages suffered by the other parties. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm Georgia remains an at-fault/tort state for auto accidents and verify current minimum liability insurance requirements */} You have several options for seeking compensation:

- **File a claim with the at-fault driver's insurance company** (a third-party claim)
- **File a claim with your own insurance company** if you have applicable coverage, such as uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage or medical payments coverage
- **File a personal injury lawsuit** in civil court against the at-fault driver and any other responsible parties

Georgia's modified comparative negligence law means that even if you were partially at fault for the accident, you may still recover damages — as long as your percentage of fault is less than 50 percent. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: O.C.G.A. § 51-12-33 — Verify comparative negligence threshold */} Your compensation will be reduced by your share of fault. Insurance companies frequently try to assign a higher percentage of fault to you than you actually bear, which is why having an experienced attorney reviewing the evidence and countering these arguments is so important.
