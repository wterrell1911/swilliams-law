## Understanding Mississippi Premises Liability Law

Mississippi premises liability law governs the responsibility that property owners and occupiers have to keep their premises reasonably safe for people who enter the property. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm current state of Mississippi premises liability law, including classification of entrants (invitees, licensees, trespassers) and applicable duty of care standards under recent case law */} The duty of care a property owner owes depends on the status of the person who was injured.

**Invitees** are people who enter property for a purpose connected to the owner's business — for example, a customer shopping in a Jackson retail store or a patient visiting a medical office. Property owners owe the highest duty of care to invitees. They must regularly inspect the premises for hazards, promptly repair or address dangerous conditions, and provide adequate warnings when a hazard cannot be immediately corrected.

**Licensees** are social guests or others who enter property with the owner's permission but not for a business purpose. Property owners must warn licensees of known dangers that are not obvious.

**Trespassers** are generally owed the least duty of care, though Mississippi law still prohibits willful or wanton injury to trespassers, and special rules may apply when children are involved. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm attractive nuisance doctrine applicability in Mississippi */}

For most slip and fall cases in Jackson — those that occur at businesses, shopping centers, restaurants, and similar commercial properties — the injured person is an invitee, and the property owner owes the highest level of care.

To establish a premises liability claim in Mississippi, you generally must prove:

1. The property owner or occupier owed you a duty of care
2. A dangerous condition existed on the property
3. The owner knew or should have known about the dangerous condition
4. The owner failed to correct the condition or adequately warn you about it
5. The dangerous condition caused your fall and resulting injuries

The "knew or should have known" element is often the most contested part of a slip and fall case. Property owners and their insurance companies will argue that they had no knowledge of the hazard, or that the condition was open and obvious and you should have avoided it. An experienced premises liability attorney knows how to counter these defenses by demonstrating patterns of negligence, inadequate inspection protocols, prior complaints about the same hazard, and other evidence that the property owner fell short of their duty.

## Common Slip and Fall Hazards in the Jackson Area

Jackson's climate, infrastructure, and commercial landscape create numerous slip and fall hazards that property owners are responsible for addressing. Common dangerous conditions that lead to falls include:

**Wet and slippery floors** — Spills in grocery stores, restaurants, and retail shops that are not promptly cleaned up or marked with warning signs are one of the most frequent causes of slip and fall injuries. Jackson's frequent rainstorms also create wet entryways and walkways that businesses must address with proper mats, drainage, and signage.

**Uneven sidewalks and walkways** — Jackson's aging infrastructure means that cracked, buckled, and uneven sidewalks are common in many parts of the city, from downtown to suburban shopping areas. Property owners and the entities responsible for maintaining public walkways have a duty to repair or warn about these hazards.

**Parking lot defects** — Potholes, crumbling asphalt, poor lighting, and absent or faded line markings in parking lots throughout Jackson create tripping and falling hazards, particularly at night. Shopping centers along I-55, retail corridors on Highway 80, and commercial properties throughout Hinds County are frequent locations for these types of falls.

**Inadequate lighting** — Poorly lit stairwells, hallways, parking garages, and outdoor walkways prevent visitors from seeing and avoiding hazards. Property owners have a responsibility to provide sufficient lighting in all areas accessible to the public.

**Loose or damaged flooring** — Torn carpet, loose tiles, uneven transitions between flooring surfaces, and worn stair treads can cause devastating falls, particularly in older commercial buildings in the Jackson metro area.

**Lack of handrails or broken handrails** — Stairways without proper handrails or with damaged handrails violate building codes and create serious fall risks, especially for elderly visitors.

**Debris and clutter in walkways** — Items left in aisles, electrical cords stretched across walkways, and merchandise stacked in pedestrian paths create tripping hazards in retail stores and workplaces throughout Jackson.

## The Injuries Slip and Falls Cause

Do not let anyone minimize what happened to you. Slip and fall injuries can be severe and life-altering, including:

- **Broken bones** — hip fractures, wrist fractures, and ankle fractures are common and can require surgery and months of rehabilitation
- **Traumatic brain injuries** — hitting your head during a fall can cause concussions, subdural hematomas, and other brain injuries with lasting cognitive effects
- **Spinal cord injuries** — falls can cause herniated discs, compression fractures, and in severe cases, paralysis
- **Torn ligaments and soft tissue damage** — ACL tears, rotator cuff injuries, and other soft tissue injuries that may require surgical repair
- **Back and neck injuries** — chronic pain conditions that can affect your ability to work and enjoy daily activities
- **Cuts, bruises, and abrasions** — which can lead to infection and scarring

These injuries can result in thousands or even hundreds of thousands of dollars in medical bills, extended time away from work, and lasting pain and disability. You should not have to bear those costs because a property owner failed to maintain safe premises.
