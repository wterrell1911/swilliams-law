## Understanding Mississippi Personal Injury Law

If you have been injured in Jackson, understanding how Mississippi personal injury law works is essential to protecting your claim.

**Statute of Limitations:** Mississippi law provides a three-year statute of limitations for most personal injury claims. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Miss. Code Ann. § 15-1-49 — verify applicability to all PI claim types discussed */} This means you generally have three years from the date of your injury to file a lawsuit. While three years may seem like a generous window, evidence disappears, witnesses forget details, and medical records become harder to obtain over time. We strongly encourage you to contact our office as soon as possible after your injury.

**Pure Comparative Negligence:** Mississippi follows a pure comparative negligence system. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Verify current status of Miss. Code Ann. § 11-7-15 and any recent case law modifications */} This is one of the more plaintiff-friendly standards in the country. Even if you were partially at fault for the accident that caused your injuries, you can still recover damages. Your compensation will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if a jury awards you $100,000 but finds you were 25 percent at fault, you would receive $75,000. This is different from many states that bar recovery if you are 50 percent or more at fault, and it is different from how Georgia handles comparative fault.

**Damages Available:** Mississippi personal injury law allows you to pursue both economic and non-economic damages. Economic damages include medical bills, future medical expenses, lost wages, lost earning capacity, and property damage. Non-economic damages cover pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life, and loss of consortium. In cases involving particularly egregious conduct, Mississippi courts may also award punitive damages to punish the wrongdoer and deter similar behavior.
