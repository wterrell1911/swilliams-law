## Mississippi Is an At-Fault State

Understanding how Mississippi's car insurance system works is critical to protecting your rights after an accident. Mississippi is an at-fault state, which means the driver who caused the collision is financially responsible for the damages. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm current Mississippi auto insurance requirements under Miss. Code Ann. § 63-15-3 and minimum liability limits */} You have three options for pursuing compensation:

1. **File a third-party claim** with the at-fault driver's insurance company
2. **File a first-party claim** with your own insurance under certain coverages like uninsured motorist or medical payments
3. **File a personal injury lawsuit** in Mississippi court against the at-fault driver or other responsible parties

Mississippi law requires all drivers to carry minimum liability insurance. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Verify current minimum limits — believed to be $25,000/$50,000/$25,000 under Miss. Code Ann. § 63-15-3 */} However, the reality is that a significant percentage of Mississippi drivers are either uninsured or underinsured. If the driver who hit you does not have adequate insurance, your own uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM) coverage may be your most important source of recovery. Attorney Williams will evaluate every potential source of compensation in your case.

Mississippi's pure comparative negligence rule also applies to car accident cases. {/* ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm application of Miss. Code Ann. § 11-7-15 to motor vehicle cases */} Even if you were partially at fault for the collision, you can still recover damages. Your recovery will be reduced by your percentage of fault, but you are not barred from making a claim. This is a critical protection for accident victims, and it is something the insurance companies will try to use against you by inflating your share of the blame.

## What to Do After a Car Accident in Jackson

The steps you take immediately after a car accident can have a significant impact on your ability to recover full compensation. Here is what we recommend:

**At the scene:**
- Call 911 and request police and medical assistance
- Seek medical attention for any injuries, even those that seem minor — adrenaline can mask serious injuries
- Move to a safe location if possible, but do not leave the scene
- Exchange names, contact information, and insurance details with the other driver or drivers
- Take photographs of the vehicles, the road, traffic signs, skid marks, debris, and your visible injuries
- Get the names and contact information of any witnesses
- File a police report with the Jackson Police Department or the Mississippi Highway Patrol, depending on the location of the accident

**In the hours and days after:**
- Follow up with your doctor and keep all medical appointments
- Do not give a recorded statement to any insurance company — theirs or yours — before speaking with a lawyer
- Keep a journal documenting your pain levels, symptoms, and how the injury is affecting your daily life
- Do not post about the accident on social media
