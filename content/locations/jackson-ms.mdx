## Mississippi-Specific Injury Law

Mississippi law differs from other states in several important ways that directly impact personal injury cases. Having an attorney who understands these nuances is essential to protecting your rights and maximizing your recovery.

### Statute of Limitations

{/* ATTORNEY REVIEW */}
Under Mississippi Code Annotated Section 15-1-49, the general statute of limitations for personal injury claims in Mississippi is **three (3) years** from the date of the injury. This gives you more time than some neighboring states, but waiting too long can still jeopardize your case. Evidence deteriorates, witnesses relocate, and memories fade. We strongly recommend contacting an attorney as soon as possible after an injury.

### Comparative Fault in Mississippi

{/* ATTORNEY REVIEW */}
Mississippi follows a **pure comparative fault** standard. This means that even if you are partially at fault for the accident that caused your injuries, you can still recover damages -- but your award will be reduced by your percentage of fault. For example, if you are found 30% at fault and your damages total $100,000, you would recover $70,000. Unlike Georgia, which bars recovery if you are 50% or more at fault, Mississippi allows recovery even at high fault percentages. This is an important distinction and one that an experienced Mississippi attorney can leverage to your benefit.

### Workers' Compensation in Mississippi

{/* ATTORNEY REVIEW */}
Mississippi's workers' compensation system is governed by the Mississippi Workers' Compensation Commission. Injured workers must report their injury to their employer within **30 days** and file a claim with the Commission within **two (2) years**. Benefits include medical treatment, temporary total disability payments, and permanent disability awards. Navigating the Mississippi workers' compensation system requires an attorney who understands the Commission's procedures and the strategies that produce results.
